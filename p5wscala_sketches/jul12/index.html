How to specify transformations

"T(tx, ty)" represents translation.
"S(s)" represents scaling.
"R(theta)" represents clockwise rotation (specified in degrees).
"X(shearX)" represents shearing in the X direction.
"Y(shearY)" represents shearing in the Y direction.

You can combine matrices together with matrix multiplication (using "m1 * m2") 
     or by-element addition (using "m1 + m2").
Examples
T(1, 1)*S(1.5) will first translate 1, 1 and then scale 1.5.
R(90)*T(1.25, 0) will rotate 90 degrees and then translate 1.25 in the x direction.
