Controls

Click and drag, use WASD or arrow keys to move the Argand diagram
Press +/- or use the scroll wheel to zoom into the Argand diagram
Use the scroll wheel in the cosine and sine graphs to scale in the time direction

Expression syntax

The expression parser understands the following things:
Numbers: 4, -6.9, 1e+4, 0.13259
Variables: use "n" to refer to the current phasor index
Arithmetic: +, -, *, /, ^
Constants: "PI/Pi/pi" and "e"
Functions: cos(), sin(), tan(), log(), sqrt()

You may put spaces between terms, group them with parentheses,
or negate them with a "-" before the term.

What's going on?

The yellow graph shows the Real (cosine) component.
The red graph shows the Imaginary (sine) component.
The blueish diagram shows the total phasor.

This applet adds phasors as they move through time. Each phasor has an index N which determines its amplitude, frequency and phase. The applet starts at the first index (N = 1), calculates its phasor's magnitude and angle, and draws it. It moves onto the next phasor (N = 2), calculates its magnitude and phase, and draws it off of the previous one. It repeats until some upper bound specified by the "How Many" number.

Here are some interesting graphs to plot:

Sawtooth Wave
Amplitude: 1/n
Frequency: n
Phase: 0

Triangle Wave
Amplitude: 1/(2n-1)^2
Frequency: 2n-1
Phase: n*PI

Square Wave
Amplitude: 1/(2n-1)
Frequency: 2n-1
Phase: 0

Euler spiral
Amplitude: 1
Frequency: 0
Phase: .01n^2
How many: 100+
